# Worked Examples

Real evaluations produced with this rubric. They show how the same method yields different verdicts,
and how the *exact variant* and *life stage* change the answer. **Always re-verify current labels —
formulas change by country and over time.**

## ✅ PASS — catz finefood Classic "Geflügel" (wet, Germany)

```
PRODUCT: catz finefood Classic Geflügel (poultry)   |   Type: wet   |   Life stage: adult   |   Origin: DE
Complete or complementary: Complete (sole diet OK)

First ingredient: Poultry 69% (muscle meat 15%, heart, stomach, liver, neck, fat)  → Tier 1 → ok
Grain: none    Legumes: none
Ash: 2.5%    Bone flag: no
Minerals: Ca not printed on this pack  P not printed  Mg n/a  Na n/a    Ca:P = unverifiable on this pack
          (brand-level catz finefood declares Ca 0.2–0.3 / P 0.15–0.25 → ~1.25:1)
Taurine: 1500 mg/kg
Red flags: none
Marketing vs reality: consistent — high named-meat content is real

VERDICT: PASS
Why:
- Named meat + full breakdown first (Tier 1), grain-free, very low ash (2.5%)
- Complete food; taurine well above minimum
- Lean (92 kcal/100g) → good for a sterilized adult, fed toward the lower portion range
Better alternative: n/a (this is a top pick)
```

## ❌ FAIL (for a sterilized adult) — Carnilove "True Fresh Turkey" Sterilised (dry)

```
PRODUCT: Carnilove True Fresh Turkey, Sterilised   |   Type: dry   |   Life stage: adult (sterilized)   |   Origin: CZ
Complete or complementary: Complete

First ingredient: "Fresh turkey 70%"  → Tier: fresh-meat caution → real backbone is dried "poultry protein 23%"
Grain: none    Legumes: heavy (yellow peas, pea protein, chickpeas, red lentils, pea flour)
Ash: 8.0%    Bone flag: borderline (high ash + high Ca; some Ca is from added eggshell)
Minerals: Ca 1.4%  P 1.1%  Mg 0.05%  Na 0.2%    Ca:P = 1.27:1 (ratio ok)
Taurine: present
Red flags: Ca and P both above adult ceilings; legume-heavy backbone
Marketing vs reality: "70% fresh meat" front → after 4–5× shrink the dominant protein is dried poultry meal + peas

VERDICT: FAIL (for a sterilized cat, on the mineral ceilings)
Why:
- Calcium 1.4% > 1.2% and phosphorus 1.1% > 1.0% — both over the adult ceilings, worse for a sterilized cat
- "Fresh 70%" is a front-label effect; real base is dried protein + several legumes
Better alternative: Carnilove Sterilised Lamb & Wild Boar (Ca 0.8 / P 0.7) or Grandorf Sterilised
```

## ✅ PASS (with caveat) — Grandorf Rabbit & Turkey Sterilised, Holistic (dry, Belgium)

```
PRODUCT: Grandorf Rabbit & Turkey, Adult Sterilised (Holistic)   |   Type: dry   |   Life stage: adult (sterilized)   |   Origin: BE
Complete or complementary: Complete

First ingredient: Dehydrated rabbit meat 25% (then dehydrated turkey 24%)  → Tier 3 → ok (best realistic for dry)
Grain: brown rice at position 3 (single-grain, not grain-free)    Legumes: minimal
Ash: 6.0%    Bone flag: no (deboned meat, no meat-and-bone meal)
Minerals: Ca 1.0%  P 0.8%  Mg 0.09%  Na n/a    Ca:P = 1.25:1

Red flags: none major
Marketing vs reality: honest — leads with dehydrated meat rather than inflating a "fresh %"

VERDICT: PASS (caveat: contains brown rice → single-grain, not grain-free)
Why:
- ~49% named dehydrated meat first, ash only 6%, Ca:P 1.25:1, low magnesium — strong for a sterilized cat
- Honest label
- If you want strictly grain-free, choose the Grandorf Fresh Sterilised line (Duck/Salmon)
Better alternative: Grandorf Fresh Sterilised (grain-free) if grain is a dealbreaker
```

## Takeaways the examples illustrate

1. **The exact variant matters.** Carnilove Sterilised Lamb & Wild Boar (Ca 0.8/0.7) and Carnilove
   True Fresh Turkey (Ca 1.4/1.1) are the *same brand* with very different results.
2. **Front labels mislead.** "70% fresh meat" and "salmon as main ingredient" both failed the
   reality check.
3. **Missing numbers = unverifiable, not "fine."** Several good foods don't print Ca/P; say so.
4. **Life stage flips verdicts.** A calcium level that fails for an adult can be acceptable for a kitten.
5. **For sterilized cats, moisture wins.** The best real answer is usually wet or wet+dry, not a fancier kibble.
